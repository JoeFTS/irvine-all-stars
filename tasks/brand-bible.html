Section 02

Logo Concept Generation

01
The Shield
★ Recommended

Visual: Shield shape containing a stylized baseball diamond, "IRVINE" arched at top, "ALL-STARS" at bottom. Stars flanking the shield.

Shape: Shield = protection, honor, tradition. Diamond = the playing field.

Emotion: Authority + tradition + pride.

Scaling: Standalone icon on hats/jerseys. Full lockup for headers. Shield-only for app icon/favicon.

Timeless: Shield crests have worked for centuries (Premier League, NFL, MLB).

02
The Star Diamond

Visual: Five-pointed star with baseball diamond integrated into its center. Negative space creates the basepaths.

Shape: Star = excellence, aspiration. Diamond = the field. Sharp, angular, modern.

Emotion: Modern + elite + aspirational.

Scaling: Star icon works at any size. Embroiders well on hats.

Timeless: Stars are universal. Geometric construction prevents trendiness.

03
The Banner

Visual: Horizontal ribbon with "IRVINE ALL-STARS" in bold type, flanked by stars. Baseball centered above.

Shape: Ribbon = championship. Horizontal = stability.

Emotion: Championship + tradition + Americana.

Scaling: Wide for jerseys/headers. Stacks for hats. Stars as micro-icons.

Timeless: Pennant shapes are embedded in baseball DNA since the 1900s.

04
The Monogram

Visual: Interlocking "IAS" in a circular badge with stars. Clean, sophisticated, premium.

Shape: Circle = unity. Interlocking letters = teamwork.

Emotion: Premium + exclusive + professional.

Scaling: Perfect for hats, jersey patches, social avatars.

Timeless: Most enduring logo format in sports (Yankees, Giants, Dodgers).

05
The Batter's Star

Visual: Batter silhouette in mid-swing, integrated into a star shape. Dynamic and action-oriented. Shape: Star = All-Stars. Batter = the sport. Diagonal lines = motion. Emotion: Action + energy + excitement. Scaling: Full mark on jerseys, simplified star for small sizes. Timeless: Action silhouettes are classic (NBA's Jerry West, MLB's batter logo).

Why The Shield Wins

The shield delivers on every brand requirement simultaneously. It communicates authority (parents trust it), tradition (it feels established, not startup-y), and honor (players feel they've earned something). It scales perfectly from favicon to billboard. It differentiates Irvine PONY All-Stars from typical clip-art-heavy youth leagues while still feeling accessible to families. The shield says: "We take this seriously. Your child is in good hands." The Star Diamond (Concept 2) is the strong runner-up and could serve as the secondary/icon mark.

Mistake to Avoid

Over-styling with script/cursive baseball fonts. Teams slap on "baseball script" fonts that feel like Little League clip-art. It screams recreational, not elite. Stick to clean, bold sans-serifs. The type should feel like a pro franchise, not a t-ball trophy shop.

Section 05

Visual Direction Guide

Aesthetic North Star
Bold. Clean. Earned.

Photography Style

Do
  • Action shots: batting, fielding, running
  • Candid team moments: huddles, high-fives
  • Natural outdoor light, golden hour heroes
  • Low angles on players (makes them powerful)
  • Show genuine emotion and diversity naturally
Don't
  • Staged stock-photo poses
  • Overly filtered/saturated images
  • Dark/moody editing
  • Adults dominating frame over kids
  • Empty fields without people

Icons & Illustration

Simple line icons (2px stroke, navy or white). Geometric, not organic. Use Lucide icons. No clip-art, no cartoon mascots, no flame/lightning effects.

Layout Principles

  • Spacing: Generous whitespace. Min 24px between sections, 16px between cards.
  • Hierarchy: One focal point per section. Headline → subtext → CTA, always.
  • Cards: Consistent padding (24-32px), subtle borders, 8-12px corners.
  • Grid: 12-column desktop, single column mobile. Max width: 1280px.
  • Rhythm: Alternate white and off-white (#F9FAFB) section backgrounds.

3 Non-Negotiable Rules

1. Never use the logo on a busy/patterned background. Always on solid navy, white, or with sufficient contrast.

2. Never use more than 2 brand colors in a single composition (plus white/gray neutrals). Navy + Red is the power combo.

3. Never use script, cursive, or decorative fonts. Oswald and Barlow only. No exceptions.
